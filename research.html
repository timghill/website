Research

Numerical modelling of ice and earth systems

An overview of the questions I work on, the methods I use, and where the tools and code live.

1. Ice-sheet dynamics under uncertain basal conditions

Ice flow at the base of an ice sheet is controlled by conditions we can rarely observe directly: the roughness of the bed, the presence of meltwater, and the strength of subglacial sediment. I run ensembles of finite-element ice-flow simulations to explore how these unknowns propagate into uncertainty in sea-level projections, focusing on fast-flowing outlet glaciers in West Antarctica.

2. Data assimilation for glacier model calibration

Rather than guessing model parameters, this work uses adjoint-based inversion to combine satellite-derived surface velocity and elevation-change data with physical models, producing calibrated simulations that are consistent with what we actually observe from space.

3. Open, reproducible HPC workflows

Large model runs are only useful if others can check and build on them. I develop containerised, version-controlled pipelines for running and post-processing simulations on shared HPC clusters, with an emphasis on workflows that a new student or collaborator can pick up without months of onboarding.
