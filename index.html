Why this matters

Modelling the ice we can't watch melt in real time

Most of the world's future sea-level rise will come from ice sheets we can only observe in fragments — a satellite pass here, a borehole there. My work fills the gaps with physics-based models, so we can turn sparse observations into predictions that matter for coastal planning and climate policy.

At a glance

Current focus

Ice-sheet dynamics under uncertain basal conditions

Using ensemble finite-element simulations to quantify how unknown bed topography and friction affect projections of ice loss in West Antarctica.

Data assimilation for glacier model calibration

Coupling satellite-derived surface velocities with adjoint-based inversion methods to better constrain hard-to-observe model parameters.

Open, reproducible HPC workflows

Building containerised, version-controlled pipelines so that large-scale model runs on shared HPC clusters are easy for others to reproduce and extend.
